Landscape & Limits
why legal AI fails — and why Algeria is the hard case
The legal domain through an AI lens

Legal meaning is not linguistic meaning

  • iStructured — codes, chapters, numbered articles; structure carries meaning
  • iiInterdependent — definitions, exceptions and cross-references live in other articles
  • iiiTime-sensitive — amendments decide which version is law today
  • ivAuthority-bound — an answer must cite the provision that makes it true
HenceStructure is an asset when machine-readable — and a liability when buried inside publication-oriented PDFs.
The problem

In law, fluency is not the standard. Justification is.

the standard of a legal answer a real legal source  ·  the correct article  ·  a valid citation  —  or a principled abstention
The low-resource condition

Algerian law as a stress test

  • ·Legal-AI research concentrates on English, well-resourced systems — LexGLUE, LegalBench, COLIEE.
  • ·Arabic legal AI is emerging (ArabLegalEval, AraLegal-BERT) — but citation-controlled reasoning for Algerian law is unexplored.
  • ·Before any model: Algeria needs infrastructure — structured corpus, article identifiers, legal links, amendment metadata, a benchmark.
The failure of current AI in law

Hallucination becomes a legal-validity problem

Failure I — Hallucination

Inventing the law

Models fabricate claims, citations and whole authorities while sounding confident — Mata v. Avianca shows fabricated citations entering real legal submissions.

Ji et al. 2023 · Mata 2023
Failure II — The attribution gap

Answering without grounding

Even a true answer is legally weightless if the system cannot show which source supports which claim. Attribution is a requirement, not a feature.

Rashkin et al. 2023
Failure III — The half-measure

Citing without justifying

Citation-aware generation is improving — but an emitted citation does not guarantee the article exists, is in force, or actually supports the claim.

Zhang et al. 2025
Fluency, truth, attribution, justification — each is a strictly stronger requirement. Law demands the last one.

Pillar II of IV · relations

Legal Knowledge Graphs — because law is relational

  • ·Provisions cite, amend, define, limit and depend on other provisions — a graph, not a list of passages.
  • ·GraphRAG: relations guide evidence expansion where text similarity is blind — exceptions and amendments never “resemble” the query. Edge et al. 2025
  • ·Temporality is the open challenge: the graph must answer “what was the law on this date?” Kucuk & Can 2025
  • ·Ours is derived from the structured corpus — not manually invented.
Pillar IIThe graph supplies what similarity cannot — the relational and temporal context of every article.
Pillar III of IV · inspectability

Argument-aware evidence — not flat text, structured units

  • ·Retrieved provisions become ADU-inspired triplets — ⟨ source · relation · statement ⟩ — instead of undifferentiated context.
  • ·Legal roles survive generation: rule, condition, exception — a fluent answer can no longer collapse them.
  • ·The same units later feed Gate III: claim-level support is checked against these grounds by NLI.
Scope — deliberately bounded

Not a full argument-mining system. No annotated Algerian argument corpus exists; this is a lightweight structuring layer, validated indirectly through the registry and the faithfulness gates.

Pillar IV of IV · control

Recursive Language Models — the orchestration layer

  • ·The RLM can decompose the question, inspect evidence, call retrieval, traverse the graph, verify support, and revise its path. Zhang et al. 2025
  • ·A control abstraction over retrieval, RAG and the KG — not a replacement for them.
  • ·Not an autonomous legal judge: every step is bounded, logged, and grounded in deterministic infrastructure.

Contribution iii — the architecture

AKN-RLM at a glance

Not a chatbot, not plain RAG — every stage is typed, bounded, and verified before delivery.
Design principle

Generative inside, deterministic outside

The LLM proposes; the infrastructure disposes — language models never define legal authority.
Orchestration

Bounded recursion — the right to look again

  • iGap-probe — after each pass, the root model names the element still unsupported — and retrieves for it specifically
  • iiCoverage floor — multi-hop & rule-application synthesise only once ≥ 4 supporting articles are held
  • iiiBounded workers — decomposition, verification & summaries delegated to Qwen3-30B, structured JSON calls
  • ivAudited — every step, retry and verdict lands in the trajectory — telemetry is part of the result
Recursion buys the right to look again — at most three times, and only through the deterministic environment.
Evidence structuring

Toulmin-style ADUs — evidence that can be audited

  • iPervasive — Gemma-4-31B extracts ADUs from every retrieved article, not just the final answer
  • iiRoles survive generation — rule, condition, exception cannot collapse into fluent prose
  • iiiFeeds gate iii — each claim is NLI-checked against its own grounds (≥ 0.55)
  • ivMakes faithfulness measurable — mirrors the benchmark’s 4-step reasoning chain — AMF 0.471
a lightweight structuring layer — not a full argument-mining system; no annotated Algerian argument corpus exists.
Flat text cannot be audited — structured evidence is what makes faithfulness measurable.

Abstention is a first-class legal answer — refusing is safer than inventing.

Reading the results

Interpretation (2/2)

  • iThe gain is architectural — same corpus, registry and indices as every tier-2 baseline
  • iiThe ceiling is retrieval — gold article in the top-10 on < 30% of queries; infrastructural, not generative
  • iiiFrench is the hard slice — an Arabic-primary system today
  • ivStrictness has a price — the gate over-rejects layman queries; wider evidence pools dilute citation discipline
  • vRefusal works — 38 / 40 infection traps correctly refused, where every baseline invents an answer
Each phase is an ablation in disguise — every layer pays for itself, though not always in F1.

Conclusion

Reliability tracks legal structure, not parametric scale

ContributionWhat it establishes
Structured Algerian corpus45 legal documents, 1963–2025 — law as machine-readable infrastructure
AlgerianLegalBench244 questions, with 67 traps, faithfulness & abstention are measurable
AKN-RLM architecturetyped handlers + KG + bounded recursion + triple gate beat every baseline tier
Zero-fabrication operation0.000 hallucinated citations, 0.000 infection — with principled abstention (F1 0.703)

The framework is contextual and methodological: built for Algeria, designed as a general recipe for low-resource, citation-faithful legal AI. No claim of legal advice; a controlled but realistic subset of Algerian positive law.

Not a chatbot that sounds legal — a system whose answers stay anchored in verifiable Algerian legal sources.
Honest boundaries

Limitations & future work

measured limits
  • iArticle-level retrieval — Recall@10 < 0.30 caps what citation recall can reach
  • iiFrench slice under-served — queries hit an Arabic-primary corpus
  • iiiAbstention precision — sometimes refuses answerable questions
  • ivCanary-gate caveat — part of its signal shares surface forms with the evaluation traps
  • vMetrics are proxies — no automated score replaces expert legal review
future work
  • iCorpus-tuned dense embedder & stronger article-level ranking
  • iiFrench coverage — parallel and French-facing legal material
  • iiiKG–retrieval interface — graph relations feeding candidate selection directly
  • ivGate ablations — no-jurisdiction-gate & no-NLI-gate runs
  • vExpert human review — on multi-hop, long-context, ambiguous slices
Every limit here is measured and stated by us first — and each one is a concrete research direction.
